Section B Short answer

Q8
Day: Earth's rotation on its axis; Month: Moon's cycle of phases; Year: Earth's revolution around the Sun (cycle of seasons).
Q9
Mark the shadow tip through the day; the shortest shadow occurs when the Sun is highest, which is local noon (midday).
Q10
A lunar calendar is based on the Moon's phase cycle (~12 lunar months per year); a solar calendar is based on Earth's revolution around the Sun (the year).

Section C Long / application

Q11
Earth's rotation on its axis gives the day (~24 hours); the Moon's cycle of phases as it revolves around Earth gives the month (~29.5 days); Earth's revolution around the Sun, marked by the cycle of seasons, gives the year (~365¼ days). These natural cycles are the basis of all calendars.
Q12
A year is about 365¼ days, so a quarter-day is left over each year; adding a leap day every 4 years keeps the calendar aligned with the seasons. Also, 12 lunar months don't exactly equal one solar year, so lunisolar calendars adjust (e.g. with extra months) to keep the Moon's months in step with the solar year.
