Why the Moon Has Phases (and Locating It)

Phases come from the Moon's position relative to the Sun — not from Earth's shadow.

Key Concept The real reason for phases

The Sun lights up one half of the Moon. As the Moon revolves around the Earth (once in ~a month), we see different amounts of that lit half — giving the phases.

•New Moon: the Moon is between Earth and Sun, so its lit half faces away from us — we see nothing. It appears closest to the Sun in the sky.
•Full Moon: the Earth is between Sun and Moon, so the whole lit half faces us. It appears farthest from the Sun (rises as the Sun sets).
Phases are NOT caused by Earth's shadow! They come from the changing Sun–Moon–Earth orientation. Earth's shadow on the Moon causes a lunar eclipse, which is different.

Eclipses

•Lunar eclipse — only on a full Moon day (Earth's shadow on Moon).
•Solar eclipse — only on a new Moon day (Moon between Earth and Sun).

They don't happen every month because the Moon's orbit is slightly tilted.

Locating the Moon

The Moon rises about 50 minutes later each day, so it can sometimes be seen in daylight. A waxing Moon is easiest to spot at sunset, a waning Moon at sunrise.

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Phases are NOT from Earth's shadow. That's a lunar eclipse. Phases are from the Sun–Moon–Earth positions.
Trap 2 — Lunar eclipse = full Moon; solar eclipse = new Moon. Don't swap.
Trap 3 — The Moon rises ~50 minutes later each day, so it doesn't always rise at sunset — that's why it can be seen by day.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Why can the Moon sometimes be seen during the day?
Step 1:The Moon rises about 50 minutes later each day.
Step 2:On many days it rises in the afternoon, so it is up while the Sun is still out.
∴ Its shifting rise time means it is often above the horizon in daylight.
Example B. A friend says the Moon's phases are caused by Earth's shadow. Correct this.
Step 1:Phases come from the changing Sun–Moon–Earth orientation as the Moon orbits.
Step 2:Earth's shadow on the Moon causes a lunar eclipse, not phases.
∴ Phases are due to position, not Earth's shadow.
