Answer Key — Interparticle Spacing, Compressibility and Diffusion

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(c) gases — gases have the largest spaces between particles.
Q2
(a) a gas — large spaces make gases easily compressible.
Q3
(b) diffusion — this spreading is diffusion.
Q4
(c) gases — gases diffuse fastest due to large spacing and fast motion.
Q5
(a) Both true, R explains A — faster-moving particles spread faster — exactly as R says.
Q6
compressed — large spacing makes gases compressible.
Q7
moving — the constant motion of particles causes diffusion.

Section B Short answer

Q8
Solids (smallest) < liquids < gases (largest).
Q9
Gases have large interparticle spaces so their particles can be pushed closer; liquids have little space, so they are almost incompressible.
Q10
The spreading of particles of one substance into another on their own; it is fastest in gases.

Section C Long / application

Q11
(i) Gas particles have very large spaces and move freely in all directions, so they spread out to occupy the entire container. (ii) The food's particles diffuse through the air; because gas particles move fast and have large spaces, the smell spreads quickly to another room.
Q12
Fill a needle-less syringe with air, block the nozzle with your thumb and push the plunger — it moves in a lot (air compresses). Repeat with water — the plunger hardly moves. Air's large interparticle spaces let particles be pushed closer; water's particles are already close with little space, so it barely compresses.
