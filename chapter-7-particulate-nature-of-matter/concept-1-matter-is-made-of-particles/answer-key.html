Section B Short answer

Q8
The smallest basic units that make up a substance, e.g. the tiny particles a piece of chalk or a grain of sugar is made of.
Q9
Sugar breaks into tiny constituent particles that spread into the interparticle spaces of water; they are too small to see, but their presence is sensed by the sweet taste.
Q10
No new substance is formed; the salt only separates into its particles and can be recovered, so it is a physical change.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Grinding shows a chalk piece can be broken into smaller and smaller specks, each still chalk — suggesting it is made of many tiny particles. Dissolving sugar shows the sugar splits into particles too small to see that spread into the spaces between water particles (sensed by taste). Both show matter is made of large numbers of extremely small particles.
Q12
There are interparticle spaces between the water particles. The dissolved sugar particles are tiny and fit into these gaps, so very little extra volume is added and the water does not overflow much.
