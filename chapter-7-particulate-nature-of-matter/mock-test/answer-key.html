Section B Short answer

Q9
Solid: definite shape and volume; Liquid: definite volume, no fixed shape; Gas: no fixed shape or volume.
Q10
Gases have large interparticle spaces, so particles can be pushed closer; solids/liquids have little space, so they are almost incompressible.
Q11
The spreading of particles of one substance into another on their own; fastest in gases.

Section C Long / application

Q12
Heating makes particles vibrate more; at the melting point they break free of fixed positions and forces weaken — solid → liquid. Further heating makes particles move fast enough to escape; forces become negligible — liquid → gas (at the boiling point).
Q13
(i) Sugar splits into tiny particles that fit into the interparticle spaces between water particles, so little extra volume is added. (ii) Incense particles diffuse through the air; gas particles move fast with large spaces, so the smell spreads quickly.
