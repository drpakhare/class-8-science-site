Answer Key — Exploring Forces

Final answers with brief reasons.

Section A Objective

Q1
(a) newton — force is measured in newtons (N).
Q2
(d) change of colour — force does not change an object's colour.
Q3
(b) opposite to the motion — friction opposes motion.
Q4
(c) gravitational — gravity is always attractive.
Q5
(b) repel — like charges repel; unlike attract.
Q6
(b) False — friction needs surfaces in contact — it is a contact force.
Q7
force — a push or pull is a force.
Q8
muscular — muscular force comes from muscle action.

Section B Short answer

Q9
A push/pull from interaction of two objects; effects: start motion, change speed, change direction, change shape (any two).
Q10
Contact forces need touching surfaces (e.g. friction); non-contact forces act at a distance (e.g. gravity).
Q11
Like charges repel, unlike charges attract; the force is the electrostatic force.

Section C Long / application

Q12
Friction — contact force (needs surfaces touching). Magnetic force — non-contact (acts at a distance, can attract or repel). Gravity — non-contact and always attractive. Friction acts only on contact; gravity is always attractive.
Q13
(i) Friction between the tyres/road (and air) acts opposite to motion, slowing and stopping it. (ii) Rubbing charges the balloon; the charged balloon exerts an electrostatic force that attracts it to the (uncharged) wall.
