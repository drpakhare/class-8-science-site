Answer Key — What Is a Force?

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(c) a push or a pull — force is a push or a pull resulting from interaction.
Q2
(b) newton (N) — force is measured in newtons (N).
Q3
(c) shape — squeezing changes the balloon's shape.
Q4
(b) an interaction between at least two objects — a force comes from two objects interacting.
Q5
(b) False — it may have balanced forces that cancel out.
Q6
direction — force can change direction of motion.
Q7
n — the newton's symbol is N.

Section B Short answer

Q8
A push or pull on an object resulting from its interaction with another object; SI unit = newton (N).
Q9
Start motion from rest, change speed, change direction of motion, change shape (any/all).
Q10
A force needs at least two objects; when one pushes another, both experience a force, and it ends when they stop interacting.

Section C Long / application

Q11
(i) decreases speed / stops it; (ii) makes it move from rest (and gives direction); (iii) changes its direction and speed.
Q12
Speed: pushing a cart makes it go faster. Direction: hitting a moving ball with a bat turns it. Shape: pressing a balloon flattens it. Each change is caused by a force.
