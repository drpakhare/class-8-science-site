Lenses

A lens is curved transparent glass that lets light through — bending it to magnify or shrink what we see.

Key Concept Convex and concave lenses

A lens is a piece of transparent material (glass/plastic) with curved surfaces. Unlike mirrors, light passes through a lens — we see things through it.

Convex lensConcave lens
Thicker in the middle, thin at edgesThicker at the edges, thin in the middle
Converges light (converging lens)Diverges light (diverging lens)
Close object → enlarged, erect (magnifier)Always erect & diminished
Water-drop lens: a rounded drop of water over text acts like a convex lens and magnifies the letters — just like a magnifying glass.

Uses

•Convex: magnifying glass, spectacles for long sight, camera and the eye's own lens.
•Concave: spectacles for short sight, peepholes, some instruments.
Mirror vs lens: a mirror reflects light (we see in it); a lens transmits light (we see through it).

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Convex lens = thick middle (converging); concave lens = thick edges (diverging). Don't swap.
Trap 2 — We see THROUGH a lens, not IN it. Lenses transmit light; mirrors reflect it.
Trap 3 — A magnifying glass is a convex lens used with the object close to it.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Which lens is used as a magnifying glass and why?
Step 1:A magnifier needs an enlarged, erect image of a close object.
Step 2:A convex lens gives this when the object is close.
∴ A convex (converging) lens is used as a magnifying glass.
Example B. How can you tell a convex lens from a concave lens by touch/look?
Step 1:Feel the thickness: convex is thicker in the middle.
Step 2:Concave is thicker at the edges.
∴ Thick centre → convex; thick edges → concave.
