Answer Key — Light: Mirrors and Lenses

Final answers with brief reasons.

Section A Objective

Q1

Q2

Q4

Q5

Q7
middle — convex lenses are thick in the middle.
Q8
convex — side mirrors are convex for a wide erect view.

Section B Short answer

Q9
(1) i = r (angle of incidence = angle of reflection). (2) The incident ray, normal and reflected ray lie in the same plane.
Q10
Concave: torch reflector / dentist's mirror (concentrates light / enlarges). Convex: side-view/road-safety mirror (wide erect view).
Q11
Convex: thicker in the middle, converges light; concave: thicker at edges, diverges light.

Section C Long / application

Q12
A convex mirror always gives an erect, diminished image and a wide field of view, so drivers see more traffic behind — ideal for side mirrors. A concave mirror converges light, so placing the bulb suitably produces a strong parallel beam — ideal for a torch/headlight reflector.
Q13
A concave mirror converges the Sun's parallel rays to a point, concentrating heat enough to burn paper. A convex lens also converges light; held close over print it forms an enlarged, erect image, so the letters look bigger. Both converge light — one by reflection, the other by transmission.
