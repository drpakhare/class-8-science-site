Section B Short answer

Q8
(1) The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection (i = r). (2) The incident ray, the normal and the reflected ray all lie in the same plane.
Q9
Plane: stay parallel; concave: converge; convex: diverge.
Q10
Incident ray: ray hitting the mirror; reflected ray: ray bouncing off; normal: line at 90° to the mirror at the point of incidence.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Shine a thin beam (through a single comb slit) onto a plane mirror on white paper. Draw the incident ray, reflected ray and the normal at the point of incidence. Measure i and r with a protractor; repeat at several angles. The angles are found equal each time, verifying i = r.
Q12
A concave mirror converges parallel sun rays to a point, concentrating light and heat there (enough to heat liquids or even burn paper) — useful for a solar concentrator. A convex mirror diverges the rays, spreading them out, so it cannot concentrate sunlight.
