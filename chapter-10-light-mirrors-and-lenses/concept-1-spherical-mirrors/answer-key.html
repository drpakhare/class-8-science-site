Q7

Section B Short answer

Q8
Concave curves inward and can give an inverted (or enlarged, when close) image; convex curves outward and always gives an erect, diminished image.
Q9
Concave: torch/headlight reflector, dentist's mirror, solar concentrator (any two). Convex: vehicle side mirror, road-safety/surveillance mirror (any two).
Q10
A plane mirror gives an erect, same-size image always; in spherical mirrors the size changes with distance, and a concave mirror can also invert the image.

Section C Long / application

Q11
The enlarged (and possibly inverted when farther) image is from a concave mirror; close up it gives an enlarged erect image. The tiny erect image is from a convex mirror, which always forms a diminished, erect image. The curvature direction (inward vs outward) causes the difference.
Q12
(i) Concave — it concentrates light into a strong beam. (ii) Convex — erect, diminished image gives a wide view to monitor a large area. (iii) Concave — held close, it gives an enlarged erect image of the face.
