Answer Key — Diseases: Causes and Types

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(b) Diabetes and asthma — diabetes and asthma are lifestyle diseases that do not spread.
Q2
(b) pathogens — germs that cause disease are pathogens.
Q3
(c) a mosquito (vector) — mosquitoes are the vector that spreads the malaria protozoan.
Q4
(b) deficiency diseases (non-communicable) — deficiency diseases come from missing nutrients and don't spread.
Q5
(a) True — chickenpox is caused by a virus and spreads from person to person.
Q6
vectors — such carrier insects are vectors.
Q7
chronic — long-lasting diseases are chronic.

Section B Short answer

Q8
Communicable: caused by pathogens and spread person to person (e.g. typhoid). Non-communicable: caused by lifestyle/diet/deficiency and do not spread (e.g. diabetes).
Q9
Through air (cough/sneeze), contaminated water/food, direct or indirect contact, and vectors (insects).
Q10
Disease-causing organisms; e.g. bacteria, viruses, fungi, protozoa, worms (any three).

Section C Long / application

Q11
They have risen because of lifestyle changes — more processed/fatty/sugary food, less physical activity, obesity and longer lifespans. Prevention: balanced diet, regular exercise, avoiding tobacco/alcohol, limiting oil and sugar (any two).
Q12
Hepatitis A and cholera (and typhoid) spread through contaminated water/food. TB and chickenpox spread through air, not water, so they would not spread this way.
