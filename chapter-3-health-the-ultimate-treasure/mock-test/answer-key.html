Section B Short answer

Q9
Symptom = felt by the patient (pain); Sign = seen/measured (fever).
Q10
Communicable are caused by pathogens and spread (e.g. typhoid); non-communicable are lifestyle/deficiency-linked and don't spread (e.g. diabetes).
Q11
They train the immune system before infection to stop disease; they cannot treat someone already ill.

Section C Long / application

Q12
Air (cough/sneeze) → mask/cover mouth; water/food → drink boiled water, eat cooked food; vectors (mosquitoes) → nets/repellents; contact (shared items) → don't share personal items (any three with a matching measure).
Q13
It is when bacteria survive and multiply despite an antibiotic. It develops from misuse — taking antibiotics when not needed or not finishing the course, letting resistant bacteria spread. Prevent it by using antibiotics only as prescribed, in the right dose and full duration, and avoiding unnecessary use.
