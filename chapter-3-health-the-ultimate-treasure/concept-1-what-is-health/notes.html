What Is Health?

Health is more than not being sick — it is physical, mental and social well-being together.

Key Concept Health is complete well-being

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), health is a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being, and not merely the absence of disease.

AspectMeans
PhysicalBody works well — good diet, exercise, sleep, hygiene.
MentalA positive, calm mind — managing stress and worry.
SocialGood relationships — friends, family, fitting in with others.

How to stay healthy

•Eat a balanced diet (fruits, vegetables, whole grains); avoid too much oil/sugar and junk food.
•Stay physically active; limit screen time; get enough sleep.
•Practise yoga / pranayama, keep clean, and say NO to tobacco, alcohol and drugs.
•Keep the environment clean — clean air and water; the AQI tells us how clean the air is.

Signs vs Symptoms

SymptomSign
What the patient feels (pain, tiredness, dizziness)What can be seen or measured (fever, rash, swelling)
Ayurveda link: true health is balance of body, mind and surroundings — through dinacharya (daily routine), ritucharya (seasonal routine), good food, sleep and a calm mind.

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Health is NOT just "no disease". The WHO definition needs all three: physical, mental and social well-being.
Trap 2 — Sign vs symptom. A symptom is felt by the patient (pain); a sign can be seen/measured (fever). Don't swap them.
Slip — mental and social health matter too: someone who eats well but is lonely is not fully healthy.
