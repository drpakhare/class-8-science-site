Answer Key — Communicable Diseases and Their Spread

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(b) Malaria — malaria is caused by a protozoan, spread by mosquitoes.
Q2
(b) bacteria — TB is a bacterial disease of the lungs.
Q3
(a) Cholera — cholera spreads via contaminated water/food.
Q4
(c) using mosquito nets/repellents and removing still water — controlling mosquitoes prevents these vector-borne diseases.
Q5
(b) False — dengue is caused by a virus (spread by mosquitoes).
Q6
worms — roundworms cause ascariasis.
Q7
mask — a mask reduces spread of air-borne pathogens.

Section B Short answer

Q8
Air-borne: cold/flu, chickenpox, measles, TB (any two). Water/food-borne: cholera, typhoid, hepatitis A (any two).
Q9
Malaria — protozoa; Dengue — virus; Cholera — bacteria; TB — bacteria.
Q10
Wash hands with soap, cover mouth/nose when coughing, wear a mask in crowds, drink clean water, eat cooked food, don't share personal items (any three).

Section C Long / application

Q11
School: ask sick students to stay home/isolate, improve ventilation/hygiene, encourage masks and handwashing. You: wash hands often, cover mouth when coughing, wear a mask, avoid sharing items, stay home if unwell (any two each).
Q12
Before: learn about the area, carry repellents/nets, follow any health advisory. During: use mosquito nets and repellents, wear long-sleeved clothes, avoid areas with still water. After: watch for fever/chills and see a doctor if symptoms appear.
