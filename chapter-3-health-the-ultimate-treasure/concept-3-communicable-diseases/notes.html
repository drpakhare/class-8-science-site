Communicable Diseases and Their Spread

Air-borne, water/food-borne and insect-borne diseases — what causes them and how to stop them.

Key Concept Know your common diseases

All communicable diseases are caused by pathogens. Here are the common ones grouped by how they spread.

Spread through air

DiseaseCaused by
Common cold & influenza (flu)Virus
Chickenpox, MeaslesVirus
Tuberculosis (TB)Bacteria

Spread through contaminated water/food

DiseaseCaused by
Hepatitis AVirus
Cholera, TyphoidBacteria
Ascariasis (roundworm)Worms

Spread by insects (vectors)

DiseaseCaused by
MalariaProtozoa
DengueVirus
Prevention toolkit: wash hands with soap, cover mouth/nose when coughing, wear a mask in crowds, don't share towels, drink boiled/clean water, eat properly cooked food, use mosquito nets/repellents, and rest at home when ill.

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Malaria is caused by a protozoan, dengue by a virus. Both are spread by mosquitoes, but the pathogens differ.
Trap 2 — TB is bacterial, not viral. Cold, flu, chickenpox, measles, dengue and hepatitis A are viral; TB, cholera and typhoid are bacterial.
Trap 3 — Match prevention to spread. Boiling water prevents water-borne disease; masks/covering the mouth prevent air-borne disease; nets prevent vector-borne disease.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Name the causal agent of (i) malaria (ii) typhoid (iii) chickenpox.
Step 1:Recall the group of each pathogen.
∴ (i) protozoa (ii) bacteria (iii) virus.
Example B. Two precautions to prevent cholera.
Step 1:Cholera spreads through contaminated water/food.
∴ Drink boiled/clean water and eat properly cooked food; maintain hygiene.
