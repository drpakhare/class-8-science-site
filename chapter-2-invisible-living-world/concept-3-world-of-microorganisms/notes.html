The World of Microorganisms

Tiny living beings, too small for the eye, found in water, soil, air — and even inside us.

Key Concept What are microorganisms?

Microorganisms (microbes) are living beings too small to see with the naked eye — you need a microscope. Micro = very small; organism = living being. They live in water, soil, air, extreme hot springs and snow, and even inside our gut.

The main groups

GroupKey featuresExample
BacteriaSingle cell; spherical, rod, comma or spiral shaped; have a nucleoidLactobacillus, Rhizobium
ProtozoaSingle cell; often move with special structuresAmoeba, Paramecium
FungiUnicellular or multicellular; have cell wall but no chlorophyllYeast (uni), Bread mould (multi)
AlgaeContain chlorophyll (green); make their own foodSpirulina, Chlorella
Viruses are special. They are microscopic and acellular (not made of cells). They multiply only inside a living host cell and can cause disease. So they are different from other microorganisms.

Activities to remember

•Pond/stagnant water (Activity 2.4) shows protozoa & algae.
•Soil suspension (Activity 2.5) shows fungi, bacteria & algae.
Magnification: a microscope makes microbes look 100–400 times bigger so we can see them.

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Viruses are not "normal" microbes. They are acellular and reproduce only inside a host. Don't call them ordinary living cells.
Trap 2 — Yeast is a fungus, not a bacterium. Yeast (unicellular fungus) and mould (multicellular fungus) are both fungi.
Trap 3 — Algae are green; fungi are not. Algae have chlorophyll and make food; fungi lack chlorophyll.
Unicellular vs multicellular: bacteria & protozoa are unicellular; fungi can be either; algae one or more cells.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Why are viruses placed apart from other microorganisms?
Step 1:They are acellular — not made of cells.
Step 2:They reproduce only inside a living host cell.
∴ Being acellular and host-dependent makes viruses different from other microbes.
Example B. A green, single-celled organism that makes its own food — which group?
Step 1:"Green" → contains chlorophyll.
Step 2:Makes own food by photosynthesis.
∴ It is an alga (algae).
