Answer Key — The World of Microorganisms

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(c) viruses — viruses are acellular and need a living host to reproduce.
Q2
(b) protozoa — both are single-celled protozoa.
Q3
(a) unicellular fungus — yeast is a single-celled fungus.
Q4
(c) algae — algae are green and photosynthesise.
Q5
(b) False — they need a microscope to be seen.
Q6
living being — organism means living being.
Q7
spiral — bacteria come in spherical, rod, comma and spiral shapes.

Section B Short answer

Q8
Bacteria (Lactobacillus), Protozoa (Amoeba), Fungi (yeast), Algae (Spirulina).
Q9
Viruses are acellular (not made of cells) and multiply only inside a living host cell.
Q10
Water, soil, air, hot springs, snow, inside the bodies of plants and animals (any two).

Section C Long / application

Q11
Pond water: protozoa (Amoeba, Paramecium) and algae. Soil suspension: fungi (mould), bacteria, algae. A microscope is essential because microbes are smaller than the eye can see; it magnifies them 100–400 times.
Q12
(i) Amoeba — unicellular, protozoa; (ii) bread mould — multicellular, fungi; (iii) Lactobacillus — unicellular, bacteria; (iv) yeast — unicellular, fungi.
