Section B Short answer

Q8
The eye can only see objects above a certain size, so microbes were invisible. The invention of the microscope (better lenses) let us see them.
Q9
Light microscope ≈ 100–400 times; electron microscope ≈ 10,00,000 (a million) times.
Q10
Many small, empty, honeycomb-like compartments; he named each one a "cell".

Section C Long / application

Q11
Hooke (1665) built a microscope, observed cork, named the "cell", and published Micrographia. Leeuwenhoek (1660s) made stronger lenses and was first to see living microbes (bacteria, blood cells) — the Father of Microbiology. Together they opened the hidden living world.
Q12
Advantage: low cost makes microscopy accessible to many people/students. Limitation: lower magnification/detail than high-powered lab microscopes. Still valuable because it lets more people observe and learn about the microscopic world.
