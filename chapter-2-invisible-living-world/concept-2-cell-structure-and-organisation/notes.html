Cell Structure, Cell Types and Levels of Organisation

The parts of a cell, how plant and animal cells differ, and how cells build up into a whole organism.

Key Concept Inside a cell

All living beings are made of cells. A typical cell has three basic parts:

PartFunction
Cell membraneOuter cover; separates one cell from another; porous — lets useful materials in and waste out.
CytoplasmJelly between membrane and nucleus; holds proteins, fats, salts; most life processes happen here.
NucleusRound structure that controls all activities of the cell and regulates growth.

Plant cell vs animal cell

FeaturePlant cellAnimal cell
Cell wallPresent (gives rigidity & strength)Absent
Plastids (e.g. chloroplast)Present (green ones do photosynthesis)Absent
VacuoleOne large vacuoleSmall or absent
Onion vs cheek: onion-peel cells (Activity 2.2) are rectangular with a cell wall; human cheek cells (Activity 2.3) are polygon-shaped with no cell wall. Stains used: safranin (red) for onion, methylene blue for cheek.

Shape follows function

•Nerve cell — long and branched, to carry messages quickly to distant parts.
•Muscle cell — spindle-shaped and flexible, to contract and relax (e.g. push food down the food pipe).

Unicellular vs multicellular

Unicellular = one cell does everything (bacteria, Amoeba). Multicellular = many cells with special jobs (plants, animals). A bacterium has no proper nucleus — only a nucleoid.

Levels of organisation

Cell → Tissue → Organ → Organ system → Organism

Similar cells form a tissue; tissues form an organ; organs working together form an organ system; all systems together make the organism.

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Cell wall vs cell membrane. All cells have a membrane. Only plant, fungal and bacterial cells have an extra cell wall. Animal cells have no cell wall.
Trap 2 — Bacteria have no true nucleus. They have a nucleoid (no nuclear membrane), unlike plant, animal, fungal and protozoan cells.
Trap 3 — Stains. Onion peel → safranin (red); cheek cells → methylene blue. Don't swap them. Glycerin is added to stop drying.
Order slip: levels go cell → tissue → organ → organ system → organism. Don't reverse them.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Give two differences between a plant cell and an animal cell.
Step 1:Cell wall: present in plant, absent in animal.
Step 2:Chloroplast & large vacuole: present in plant, absent/small in animal.
∴ Plant cells have a cell wall and chloroplasts; animal cells do not.
Example B. How does the shape of a nerve cell suit its function?
Step 1:Its job is to carry messages around the body.
Step 2:Being long and branched lets it reach distant parts quickly.
∴ The long, branched shape helps it pass messages fast.
