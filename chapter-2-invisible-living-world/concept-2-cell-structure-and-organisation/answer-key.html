Answer Key — Cell Structure, Cell Types and Levels of Organisation

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(c) cell wall — the cell wall is present in plant cells, absent in animal cells.
Q2
(b) nucleus — the nucleus regulates activities and growth.
Q3
(b) a nucleoid (no nuclear membrane) — bacteria have a nucleoid, not a well-defined nucleus.
Q4
(b) Cell &rarr; Tissue &rarr; Organ &rarr; Organ system &rarr; Organism — cells build up to tissue, organ, organ system, then organism.
Q5
(a) Both true, R explains A — the spindle shape suits its contracting function — shape follows function.
Q6
safranin — safranin gives onion cells a pink/red colour.
Q7
unicellular — one-celled organisms are unicellular.

Section B Short answer

Q8
Cell membrane — encloses the cell, lets materials in/out; Cytoplasm — where most life processes occur; Nucleus — controls activities and growth.
Q9
Onion cells are rectangular with a cell wall; cheek cells are polygonal with no cell wall (onion stained with safranin, cheek with methylene blue).
Q10
Cell → Tissue → Organ → Organ system → Organism.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Differences: plant has cell wall / animal does not; plant has chloroplasts / animal does not; plant has one large vacuole / animal small or none. Similarities: both have a cell membrane, cytoplasm and a nucleus.
Q12
Nerve cells are long and branched to carry messages to distant parts quickly; muscle cells are spindle-shaped and flexible to contract and relax (e.g. push food). Each special shape suits the job the cell does.
