Final answers with brief reasons.

Q1

Q2

Q3

Q4

nitrogen — Rhizobium fixes atmospheric nitrogen.

Section B Short answer

Q9
Plant cell has a cell wall and chloroplasts; animal cell lacks both (plant has one large vacuole, animal small/none).
Q10
They are acellular and multiply only inside a living host cell.
Q11
(i) Yeast → carbon dioxide; (ii) Lactobacillus → lactic acid.

Section C Long / application

Q12
Soil bacteria and fungi decompose dead plant/animal matter into simple, nutrient-rich manure that returns nutrients to soil; Rhizobium in legume root nodules fixes nitrogen from air, so crop rotation with legumes naturally enriches the soil.
Q13
Hooke named the cell from cork and wrote Micrographia (1665); Leeuwenhoek first saw living microbes (Father of Microbiology). Light microscope ≈ 100–400×; electron microscope ≈ 10,00,000×.
