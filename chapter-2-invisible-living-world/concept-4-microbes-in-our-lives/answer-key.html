Answer Key — Microbes in Our Lives

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(b) yeast — yeast respires and releases CO₂ that makes dough fluffy.
Q2
(c) Lactobacillus — Lactobacillus ferments milk, producing lactic acid (sour curd).
Q3
(b) trapping nitrogen from air to enrich soil — they fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
Q4
(b) high salt/sugar stops microbes growing — high salt/sugar acts as a preservative; microbes can't grow.
Q5
(a) Both true, R explains A — decomposition recycling waste is exactly how microbes clean the environment.
Q6
biogas — biogas is mainly methane and carbon dioxide.
Q7
decomposition — the process is decomposition; the product is manure.

Section B Short answer

Q8
Yeast makes dough rise by producing carbon dioxide (and a little alcohol); Lactobacillus turns milk into curd by producing lactic acid.
Q9
Soil bacteria/fungi decompose dead matter into nutrient-rich manure; Rhizobium in legume root nodules fixes nitrogen from air, enriching the soil.
Q10
Salting/adding sugar (high concentration stops microbes growing) and refrigeration (cold slows microbes); also drying removes the moisture microbes need.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Yeast in B respires the sugar and releases carbon dioxide, which inflates the balloon (A has no yeast, so no gas). To prove it is CO₂, pass the gas through lime water — lime water turns milky.
Q12
Friends: make curd/bread (Lactobacillus, yeast), enrich soil/clean waste (Rhizobium, decomposers), give oxygen (algae) — any two. Foes: spoil food (mould on fruit) and cause diseases in plants, animals and humans — any two.
