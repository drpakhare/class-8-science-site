Section B Short answer

Q8
When current flows through a conductor, its resistance turns electrical energy into heat, warming the conductor. Appliances: electric iron, kettle, heater, immersion rod, hair dryer (any two).
Q9
The material, thickness and length of the wire, the amount of current, and the time for which current flows (any three).
Q10
Copper has low resistance, so it carries current without much heating; nichrome has high resistance, so it produces a lot of heat — ideal for a heating element.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Both effects. The wire warming shows the heating effect (resistance → heat); the compass deflection shows the magnetic effect (current produces a magnetic field). A single current can show both.
Q12
They give clean heat with no smoke/soot (better indoor air and health), are easy to control by switching on/off and adjusting, need no storage of fuel, and reduce indoor air pollution — though they need electricity and safe wiring.
