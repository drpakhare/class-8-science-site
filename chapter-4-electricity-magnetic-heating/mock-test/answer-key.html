Answer Key — Electricity: Magnetic and Heating Effects

Final answers with brief reasons.

Section A Objective

Q1
(b) magnetic — current produces a magnetic field — the magnetic effect.
Q2
(b) increase the number of turns — more turns (and more current, and an iron core) make it stronger.
Q3
(a) has high resistance and gives more heat — high resistance produces more heat for a given current.
Q4
(b) chemical reactions inside it — a chemical reaction inside the cell makes the current.
Q5
(b) carbon rod — the central carbon rod is positive; the zinc container is negative.
Q6
(a) True — Oersted, in 1820.
Q7
electrolyte — the conducting liquid is the electrolyte.
Q8
electromagnet — it is an electromagnet.

Section B Short answer

Q9
Increase the current (more cells) and increase the number of turns (or add an iron core).
Q10
Copper has low resistance (carries current with little heating); nichrome has high resistance (produces a lot of heat).
Q11
(i) Electric iron — heating effect; (ii) electric bell — magnetic effect.

Section C Long / application

Q12
Wind insulated wire on an iron nail, connect to a cell; the nail attracts iron clips and deflects a compass when current flows, showing a magnetic field. Stronger by: more current (more cells), more turns, and using an iron core.
Q13
Heating effect: the wire's resistance converts electrical energy into heat, warming it. Magnetic effect: the current produces a magnetic field that deflects the compass needle. One current can show both effects.
