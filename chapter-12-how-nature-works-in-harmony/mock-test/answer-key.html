Final answers with brief reasons.

Section B Short answer

Q9
Biotic = living (plants, animals); abiotic = non-living (water, soil, sunlight).
Q10
A population is one kind of organism in a habitat; a community is all the different populations together.
Q11
Habitat loss (forest shrinkage) leaves food/water scarce, so they enter farms; corridors connect forests so they can move safely without conflict.

Section C Long / application

Q12
Fish eat dragonfly larvae, so removing fish increases dragonflies. Dragonflies eat bees/butterflies, so more dragonflies means fewer pollinators, so fewer flowers are pollinated and fewer seeds form. Thus fish numbers indirectly affect plant seed production — showing interdependence.
Q13
Cutting forests and changing land use shrink habitats; animals lose food and shelter and enter human areas, causing conflict and damage. Protection: conserve forests and create wildlife corridors connecting habitats so animals can move safely, keeping nature in balance.
