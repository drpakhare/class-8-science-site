Section B Short answer

Q8
The place where an organism lives, e.g. a pond, a forest (or even a tree's bark).
Q9
Biotic = living things (plants, animals, microbes); abiotic = non-living things (water, soil, air, sunlight).
Q10
Biotic needs: food from small plants and animals; abiotic needs: oxygen, water, space and shelter.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Biotic: algae/small animals (food), other organisms. Abiotic: water, oxygen, sunlight, soil. The fish depends on biotic components for food and on abiotic components like water and dissolved oxygen for breathing and living space — both are needed for survival.
Q12
They share the same habitat but use it at different times and in different ways — the rodent is active in the warmer day, the snake at night — so they face different conditions and don't directly compete at the same time, allowing them to coexist in harmony.
