Section B Short answer

Q8
A population is a group of the same kind of organism in a habitat at a given time; a community is all the different populations sharing that habitat.
Q9
Biotic-biotic: a snake eats fish. Biotic-abiotic: earthworms live in moist soil (or a fish takes oxygen from water).
Q10
The transfer of pollen from the stamens to the carpels (by insects, birds, wind or water); it is essential for the formation of fruits and seeds.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Fish eat dragonfly larvae, so a pond with fish has fewer dragonflies. Dragonflies eat bees and butterflies, so fewer dragonflies means more of these pollinators. More pollinators carry more pollen between flowers, so nearby plants produce more seeds and more flowers. This shows how biotic components are interconnected.
Q12
If all organisms were the same, they would all need the same food, water and space, leading to intense competition and scarcity. A variety of organisms use resources differently and depend on one another (food, pollination, decomposition), keeping the habitat balanced and sustainable.
