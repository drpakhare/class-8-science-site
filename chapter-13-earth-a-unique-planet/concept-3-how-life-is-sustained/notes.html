How Life Is Sustained on Earth

Air, water and sunlight work together — through photosynthesis and respiration — to keep life going.

Key Concept The connections that keep life going

Earth has the right conditions for life, but it is the connections between living and non-living things that sustain it.

The oxygen–carbon dioxide cycle

•Plants use sunlight, carbon dioxide and water to make food by photosynthesis, releasing oxygen.
•Animals and plants use that oxygen for respiration, releasing carbon dioxide — which plants use again.

Sunlight, heat and water

•Sunlight powers photosynthesis and warms the Earth; the mild greenhouse effect keeps it warm enough for liquid water.
•Water covers about 70% of Earth and is essential for all life.
Everything connects: air, water, soil, sunlight and living things interact — the same idea of interdependence you met in the ecosystem chapter.
Our responsibility: human activities can disturb these balances (e.g. raising CO₂ warms the planet), so we must protect Earth's delicate conditions.

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Photosynthesis releases oxygen; respiration uses it. Plants do both; the two processes balance the gases.
Trap 2 — A mild greenhouse effect is helpful — it keeps Earth warm; only too much is harmful.
Trap 3 — Life depends on links, not single factors. Air, water and sunlight work together to sustain life.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. How do photosynthesis and respiration keep the air balanced?
Step 1:Plants take in CO₂ and release O₂ during photosynthesis.
Step 2:Living things use O₂ and release CO₂ during respiration.
∴ The two processes recycle oxygen and carbon dioxide, keeping the air fit for life.
Example B. Why is the greenhouse effect important for sustaining life?
Step 1:It traps some of the Sun's heat in the atmosphere.
Step 2:This keeps the temperature warm enough for water to stay liquid.
∴ A mild greenhouse effect keeps Earth warm enough for life.
