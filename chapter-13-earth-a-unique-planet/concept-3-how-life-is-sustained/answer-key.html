Section B Short answer

Q8
Photosynthesis takes in CO₂ and releases O₂; respiration uses O₂ and releases CO₂. Together they recycle the two gases, keeping the air suitable for life.
Q9
Water is essential for all life processes; it covers about 70% of Earth's surface (making Earth the Blue Planet).
Q10
It supplies oxygen for respiration, and its mild greenhouse effect keeps Earth warm enough for liquid water (and the ozone layer blocks UV).

Section C Long / application

Q11
Sunlight lets plants carry out photosynthesis using water (from soil) and carbon dioxide (from air) to make food, releasing oxygen. Animals and plants use this oxygen for respiration, releasing CO₂ that plants reuse. Sunlight also warms Earth, and the mild greenhouse effect keeps water liquid. Thus air, water and sunlight are linked in cycles that keep life going.
Q12
Habitability factors: the right distance from the Sun (liquid water/habitable zone), the right size (gravity to hold an atmosphere), an atmosphere with oxygen and an ozone layer, and a magnetic field that shields from harmful particles. Sustaining cycles: photosynthesis and respiration recycle oxygen and carbon dioxide, water cycles through the environment, and sunlight powers life — all interconnected, which is why we must protect Earth's balance.
