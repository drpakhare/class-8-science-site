Size, Atmosphere and Magnetic Field

Earth's size lets it hold an atmosphere; oxygen, the ozone layer and the magnetic field together shield and sustain life.

Key Concept More than just distance

The right size

Earth is the right size so its gravity can hold on to an atmosphere.

•Too small → weak gravity, gases escape (Mars's atmosphere is ~100× thinner; Mercury has none).
•Too large → gravity so strong it could crush us.

The atmosphere and ozone

•Oxygen in the air lets almost all life breathe.
•Some oxygen forms ozone (a three-atom oxygen molecule), making the ozone layer that blocks harmful ultraviolet (UV) rays.

The magnetic field

The movement of molten iron in Earth's core makes Earth behave like a giant magnet. The magnetic field acts as a shield, deflecting harmful particles — cosmic rays (from space) and the solar wind (from the Sun) — protecting the atmosphere and life.

Together: right position + right size + atmosphere + ozone + magnetic field make Earth able to support and protect life.

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Gravity (from the right size) holds the atmosphere. A too-small planet loses its gases to space.
Trap 2 — Ozone blocks UV rays. Ozone is made of oxygen but is a different molecule (three oxygen atoms).
Trap 3 — The magnetic field shields from cosmic rays/solar wind, not from meteors. It comes from molten iron in the core.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Why does Earth hold an atmosphere but Mercury does not?
Step 1:Holding an atmosphere needs enough gravity.
Step 2:Earth's size gives enough gravity; small Mercury's is too weak, so its gases escaped.
∴ Earth's larger size and stronger gravity hold the atmosphere.
Example B. How does the ozone layer protect life?
Step 1:Ozone forms a layer high in the atmosphere.
Step 2:It absorbs harmful ultraviolet (UV) rays from the Sun.
∴ It shields living cells from damaging UV radiation.
