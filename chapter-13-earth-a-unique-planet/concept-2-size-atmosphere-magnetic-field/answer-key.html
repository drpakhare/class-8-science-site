Section B Short answer

Q8
It gives just enough gravity to hold an atmosphere — too small and gases escape, too large and gravity could be crushing.
Q9
It blocks harmful ultraviolet (UV) rays from the Sun, protecting living cells.
Q10
It acts as a shield, deflecting harmful cosmic rays and the solar wind, protecting the atmosphere and life.

Section C Long / application

Q11
(1) The right size gives enough gravity to hold an atmosphere. (2) The atmosphere provides oxygen to breathe and an ozone layer that blocks harmful UV rays. (3) The magnetic field (from molten iron in the core) deflects cosmic rays and the solar wind, protecting the atmosphere and life.
Q12
It provides oxygen for respiration of almost all living things. It also forms an ozone layer that absorbs harmful UV rays, shielding living cells; and the greenhouse gases keep the temperature warm enough for liquid water.
