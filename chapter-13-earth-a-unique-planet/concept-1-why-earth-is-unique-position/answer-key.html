Section B Short answer

Q8
It is just right — not so close that water evaporates nor so far that it freezes — so water stays liquid, which is essential for life.
Q9
The range of distances from a star over which water can remain in the liquid state; also called the Goldilocks zone.
Q10
Gases like carbon dioxide trap some of the Sun's heat, keeping Earth's temperature just right for liquid water; without it Earth would be too cold.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Earth is at the right distance from the Sun and has a mild greenhouse effect, keeping temperatures suitable for liquid water and life. Venus, though farther from the Sun than Mercury, has a thick carbon dioxide atmosphere that traps heat strongly (runaway greenhouse effect), making it the hottest planet — far too hot for liquid water or life.
Q12
Probably not, because beyond the habitable zone it is too cold — the water would freeze and there would not be the right temperature for plant life processes. Liquid water and a suitable temperature are essential, which such a planet lacks.
