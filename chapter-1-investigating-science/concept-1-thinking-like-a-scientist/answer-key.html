Answer Key — Thinking Like a Scientist

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(b) Does thicker dough take longer to puff up? — it can be tested by changing thickness and measuring time.
Q2
(a) observation — it is directly seen, with no reasoning added.
Q3
(c) The puri puffed up in 8 seconds. — it has a number and a unit.
Q4
(a) Both A and R true, and R explains A — "because steam formed" is reasoning, so it is an inference — exactly what R states.
Q5
(b) False — science can be done anywhere, even a kitchen.
Q6
quantitative — number + unit makes it quantitative.
Q7
kite — the kite stands for curiosity taking flight.

Section B Short answer

Q8
Observation = what you directly notice (e.g. "the puri is golden"). Inference = an explanation from observations (e.g. "it is cooked because it is golden").
Q9
Qualitative: "the phulka browned/swelled." Quantitative: "it swelled in 4 seconds."
Q10
e.g. "Does hotter oil make a puri puff in less time?" — names what to change and what to measure.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Question: "Is the tyre losing air through the valve?" Change: apply soapy water to different parts / try a new valve. Observe/measure: where bubbles form, or pressure after one night. (Any testable question + one variable + one measurement.)
Q12
Observations: "it sank then rose," "it puffed in 6 seconds," "the bottom side was thinner" (any two). Inference: "probably because it touched the hot pan first."
