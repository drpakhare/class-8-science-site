Solubility of Gases

Gases dissolve in water too — and the dissolved oxygen that keeps fish alive shrinks as water warms.

Key Concept Gases dissolve in liquids

Gases also dissolve in water. Oxygen dissolves only to a small extent, but this dissolved oxygen sustains all aquatic life (fish, plants and other organisms). Gas dissolved evenly in water makes a uniform mixture.

Effect of temperature on gas solubility

Opposite to solids! The solubility of gases generally decreases as temperature increases.
•Cold water holds more dissolved oxygen → good for aquatic life.
•Warm water holds less oxygen → can harm fish (a reason warming water/thermal pollution is dangerous).
Everyday clue: a cold fizzy drink fizzes more when it warms up — the dissolved gas escapes as solubility drops.

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Gas solubility DECREASES with temperature (the opposite of most solids). Don't mix them up.
Trap 2 — Cold water holds MORE oxygen. So aquatic life is better supported in cooler water.
Trap 3 — Dissolved oxygen is small but vital. Even a little dissolved O₂ keeps fish alive.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Why can fish survive better in cold water than in very warm water?
Step 1:Gas solubility decreases as temperature rises.
Step 2:Cold water holds more dissolved oxygen for the fish to breathe.
∴ More dissolved oxygen in cold water supports aquatic life.
Example B. Why does a fizzy drink go flat faster when warm?
Step 1:The drink contains dissolved carbon dioxide.
Step 2:Warming lowers gas solubility, so the gas escapes.
∴ Less gas stays dissolved when warm, so it goes flat.
