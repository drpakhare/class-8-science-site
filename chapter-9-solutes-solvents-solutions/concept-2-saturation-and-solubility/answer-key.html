Section B Short answer

Q8
An unsaturated solution can dissolve more solute at that temperature; a saturated solution cannot — extra solute settles at the bottom.
Q9
The maximum amount of a solute that dissolves in a fixed quantity of solvent at a given temperature; for most solids, solubility increases with temperature.
Q10
3 spoons in 100 mL — the same solute is in less solvent, so the solute per mL is higher.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Make a saturated solution of baking soda in water at 20°C (until some stays undissolved). Heat it to 50°C while stirring — the leftover dissolves; add more until saturated again, then heat to 70°C and it dissolves more. This shows hotter water dissolves more solid, so solubility increases with temperature.
Q12
A solution is saturated only at a particular temperature. If you raise the temperature, solubility increases, so it can dissolve more solute and becomes unsaturated. So a saturated solution can dissolve more if heated.
