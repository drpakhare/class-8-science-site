Section B Short answer

Q8
Solute = the substance that dissolves (salt); solvent = the substance it dissolves in (water); solution = the uniform mixture formed (salt water).
Q9
The liquid present in the smaller amount is the solute; the one in the larger amount is the solvent.
Q10
Salt dissolves evenly to give a uniform mixture (solution); sand stays separate, forming a non-uniform mixture.

Section C Long / application

Q11
(i) When a solid dissolves in a liquid, the solid is the solute and the liquid the solvent (e.g. sugar = solute, water = solvent). (ii) When two liquids mix, the one in the smaller amount is the solute and the one in the larger amount is the solvent (e.g. a little acetic acid in much water → acid = solute, water = solvent).
Q12
The sugar and salt dissolve evenly throughout the water, so their particles are uniformly spread; every sip has the same amount of each. This shows ORS is a uniform mixture — a solution.
