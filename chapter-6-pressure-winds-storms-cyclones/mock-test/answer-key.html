Answer Key — Pressure, Winds, Storms, and Cyclones

Final answers with brief reasons.

Section A Objective

Q1
(b) area — pressure is force per unit area.
Q2
(b) 30 Pa — P = 90 ÷ 3 = 30 Pa.
Q3
(b) height — it depends on the height/depth, not width.
Q4
(b) high to low pressure — air moves high→low pressure.
Q5
(a) eye — the eye is the calm, lowest-pressure centre.
Q6
(b) False — fast winds have reduced (lower) pressure.
Q7
pascal — pressure is measured in pascals.
Q8
thunderstorm — that is a thunderstorm.

Section B Short answer

Q9
Pressure = force per unit area; P = 120 ÷ 4 = 30 Pa.
Q10
Liquid pressure rises with height, so a high tank gives strong tap flow; water pressure is greatest near the dam's bottom, so a broad base withstands it.
Q11
Sea breeze: sea→land (day); land breeze: land→sea (night).

Section C Long / application

Q12
(i) The sharp edge has a smaller area, so for the same force the pressure is higher and it cuts easily. (ii) Pressing the sucker pushes out air, lowering the inside pressure; the higher outside atmospheric pressure holds it to the wall.
Q13
Warm moist air rises from warm ocean water; condensation releases heat, warming the air so it rises more, forming a very low-pressure centre. Surrounding air rushes in and Earth's rotation makes it spin, forming the cyclone. Over land, the warm moist air supply is cut off, so it weakens.
