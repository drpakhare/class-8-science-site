Pure Substances and Elements

A pure substance is made of one kind of particle; elements are pure substances that can't be broken down further.

Key Concept Pure substances and elements

In science, a pure substance is made of only one type of particle and cannot be separated into other substances by any physical process. (This is stricter than the everyday meaning of "pure" = unadulterated.)

Elements

An element is a pure substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances. Elements are the building blocks of all matter. Each element is made of identical particles called atoms, different from every other element's atoms.

•Examples: hydrogen, oxygen, carbon, sulfur, gold, silver, iron.
•Atoms often join to form a molecule (e.g. two hydrogen atoms → one hydrogen molecule).

Metals, non-metals and metalloids

MetalsNon-metalsMetalloids
Gold, silver, iron, magnesium, aluminiumCarbon, sulfur, hydrogen, oxygenSilicon, boron (in-between)
Adulteration is illegally adding cheaper/poor substances to a product — a "pure" packet of food may still be a mixture to a scientist if it has more than one substance.

Common Traps Where students lose marks

Trap 1 — Element = cannot be broken down further. Hydrogen and oxygen are elements; water is not (it splits into them).
Trap 2 — Atom vs molecule. An atom is the smallest particle of an element; a molecule is two or more atoms joined together.
Trap 3 — "Pure" in science ≠ "pure" on a label. Science: one kind of particle. Everyday: unadulterated.
Metalloids (silicon, boron) have properties between metals and non-metals.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Why is oxygen called an element but water is not?
Step 1:Oxygen cannot be broken into simpler substances.
Step 2:Water can be split (by electricity) into hydrogen and oxygen.
∴ Oxygen is an element; water is made of two elements.
Example B. Classify: gold, sulfur, silicon.
Step 1:Gold → metal; sulfur → non-metal.
Step 2:Silicon has in-between properties → metalloid.
∴ Gold = metal, sulfur = non-metal, silicon = metalloid.
