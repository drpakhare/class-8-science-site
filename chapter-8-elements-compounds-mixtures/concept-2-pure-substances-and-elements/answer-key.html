Answer Key — Pure Substances and Elements

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(b) cannot be broken into simpler substances — elements cannot be broken down into simpler substances.
Q2
(a) atom — each element is made of identical atoms.
Q3
(b) carbon, sulfur, oxygen — carbon, sulfur and oxygen are non-metals.
Q4
(c) metalloids — elements between metals and non-metals are metalloids.
Q5
(a) Both true, R explains A — since water splits into two elements, it isn't an element — as R explains.
Q6
molecule — joined atoms form a molecule.
Q7
particle — a pure substance has one kind of particle.

Section B Short answer

Q8
In science, a pure substance is made of only one type of particle and can't be split physically. Everyday 'pure' means unadulterated, but such a product may still be a mixture of several substances.
Q9
A pure substance that cannot be broken into simpler substances; metals: gold, iron (etc.); non-metals: carbon, sulfur, oxygen (any two each).
Q10
An atom is the smallest particle of an element; a molecule is two or more atoms joined together.

Section C Long / application

Q11
All are elements (cannot be broken into simpler substances). Gold — metal; oxygen — non-metal; silicon — metalloid; sulfur — non-metal. An element is a pure substance made of one kind of atom.
Q12
To a scientist 'pure' means a single kind of particle, so ghee (many substances) would be a mixture, not pure. On the label, 'pure' means unadulterated — nothing cheaper or harmful has been added — which is the everyday meaning and acceptable for food.
