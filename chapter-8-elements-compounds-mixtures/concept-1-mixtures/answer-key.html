Answer Key — Mixtures

Final answer with a one-line reason. Full methods are in the Notes' worked examples.

Section A Objective

Q1
(b) keep their own properties — a mixture's components keep their own properties; no reaction occurs.
Q2
(c) sugar dissolved in water — a sugar solution is evenly mixed — uniform.
Q3
(b) copper and zinc — brass = copper + zinc (bronze = copper + tin).
Q4
(c) carbon dioxide — CO₂ forms calcium carbonate, turning lime water milky.
Q5
(b) False — air is a uniform mixture, not a compound.
Q6
alloy — such metal mixtures are alloys.
Q7
components — the parts of a mixture are its components.

Section B Short answer

Q8
A combination of two or more substances that keep their own properties; uniform: sugar solution; non-uniform: sand in water (or salad).
Q9
Its gases are evenly mixed and cannot be seen separately; mainly nitrogen (~78%), oxygen, argon, carbon dioxide and water vapour.
Q10
Stainless steel: iron, nickel, chromium, carbon; brass: copper + zinc; bronze: copper + tin.

Section C Long / application

Q11
Uniform mixtures have evenly distributed components that can't be seen separately (sugar solution, air); non-uniform have visible/uneven components (salad, sand in water). Alloys (steel, brass, bronze) are uniform mixtures of metals — they look the same throughout.
Q12
Leave colourless lime water (calcium hydroxide) exposed to air; it turns milky. Word equation: Calcium hydroxide + Carbon dioxide → Calcium carbonate + Water. The milky calcium carbonate shows CO₂ is present in air.
